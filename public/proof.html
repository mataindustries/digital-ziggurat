A readable fallback and proof page for Sergio Mata.

What this is

The Ziggurat is a public proof-of-work layer for Sergio Mata - a place to show process, experiments, shipped artifacts, and the uncomfortable middle between idea and execution.

Why it exists

Most portfolios only show polished outcomes. The Ziggurat exists to show the thinking, scars, iterations, and proof behind the work.

What it proves

  • AI-assisted building can be more than prompting
  • Taste and judgment still matter
  • Fast shipping creates leverage
  • Public artifacts are stronger than claims
  • The weird layer is often where the signal lives

How it was built

The Ziggurat is a Vite and React site deployed as a static Cloudflare Pages project, with public metadata, plain HTML proof files, and AI-readable documents alongside the visual experience. The point is not just the interface; it is the habit of turning work into inspectable artifacts.

How it connects

The main portfolio shows polish and professional proof. The Ziggurat shows depth, process, range, and experimentation.
